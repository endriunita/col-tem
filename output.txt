This is a genetic algorithm developed for the TSP problem. The first population is generated randomly, after which I prepare new, subsequent populations
by crossbreeding, mutating and randomly generating chromosomes. I generate half of the new population by crossbreeding the first half of the old population,
a quarter of the new population by mutating a quarter of the old population (the parents used in mutation are not the ones used in crossbreeding)
and the remaining quarter of the new populatin I generate randomly.

The crossovering is done by selecting 2 parents, generating 2 random indexes resembling positions from the first parent, and selecting the numbers that are between the indexes.
After making the selection I go through each element of the selection and delete it from the second parent, after which I append the whole selection to the end of the
second parent, resulting in a child.

The mutation is done by selecting 2 indexes resembling positions inside a chromosome, and reversing the elements between the indexes.

From what I observed, the bigger the population size and higher the number of generations the better the results are. I have noticed this
because the 2 lowest distances ( ~129900 ) are obtained by coupling the population size of 500 with 1000 generations.
